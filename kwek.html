Quail Egg Street Food

Home Recipes

Ingredients

  • 36 pieces quail eggs, hard-boiled and peeled
  • 1 ¼ cup flour, divided
  • 1 packet Magic Sarap seasoning powder (8 grams or about 1 ½ tsp)
  • 1 tsp orange food coloring powder or annatto powder
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • Canola oil, for frying

Vinegar Sauce

  • ½ cup vinegar
  • ½ cup ketchup
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 4 Thai chili peppers, stemmed and chopped

Special Sauce

  • 2 cups water
  • ⅓ cup soy sauce
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1 head garlic, peeled and minced
  • ¼ cup shallots, peeled and chopped
  • 4 Thai chili peppers, stemmed and chopped
  • 1 tbsp flour
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp pepper

Instructions

  1. In a deep pan over medium heat, heat about 2 inches of oil to 350°F.
  2. In a large bowl, combine 1 cup flour, Magic Sarap, food coloring, baking powder, and salt. Stir to mix evenly.
  3. Add water and stir until the batter is smooth and thick.
  4. In a shallow dish, place the remaining ¼ cup flour. Individually dredge each egg in flour, then dip into the batter to coat fully.
  5. Carefully place battered eggs into the hot oil. Repeat with remaining eggs.
  6. Deep-fry for 2-4 minutes, turning as needed, until golden brown and crisp.
  7. Remove eggs from oil with a slotted spoon and drain on a wire rack over a baking sheet. Serve immediately with a dipping sauce of choice.

For the Vinegar Sauce

  1. Combine vinegar, ketchup, brown sugar, soy sauce, and chopped chili peppers in a saucepan.
  2. Over medium heat, bring the mixture to a simmer, stirring occasionally, until sugar is dissolved (about 3-5 minutes).

For the Special Sauce

  1. Combine water, soy sauce, garlic, shallots, chili peppers, brown sugar, flour, cornstarch, salt, and pepper in a saucepan. Stir until smooth.
  2. Over medium heat, bring the mixture to a boil, stirring regularly for about 3-5 minutes or until thickened. The sauce will thicken further as it cools.
